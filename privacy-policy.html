5. Accessibility Service Usage

Ravia uses Android's Accessibility Service to provide its core content blocking functionality. This section explains exactly how and why we use this permission.

Why we need Accessibility Service: To detect when you navigate to websites you've chosen to block, so we can help you stay focused on your goals.

5.1 What the Accessibility Service Does

  • Monitors the currently active app and browser URL bar
  • Compares URLs against your personal block list (stored locally)
  • Displays an intervention screen when a blocked site is detected
  • All processing happens entirely on your device

5.2 What the Accessibility Service Does NOT Do

  • Does NOT collect or transmit your browsing history
  • Does NOT record which websites you visit
  • Does NOT send any data to our servers
  • Does NOT access your passwords, messages, or other sensitive content
  • Does NOT run when the app is disabled

5.3 Your Control

You can disable the Accessibility Service at any time through your device's Settings → Accessibility → Ravia. The app's other features (notes, tracking, insights) will continue to work without it.
